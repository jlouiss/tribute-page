Nikola Tesla

Let the future tell the truth, and evaluate each one according to his work and accomplishments.
The present is theirs; the future, for which I have really worked, is mine.

  • 1856 - Born in Smilijan, Austrian Empire
  • 1875 - Begins studying at the Austriac Polytechnic in Graz, doesn't pass third year
  • 1878 - Leaves the studies, starts working as a draftsman
  • 1881 - Moves to Budapest, works as a draftsman at the Central Telegraph Office
  • 1884 - Moves to New York, starts working for Edison
  • 1886 - Stops working for Edison and founds the Tesla Electric Light & Manifacturing Company. Soon after investors make him lose all of his money
  • 1887 - Invents an AC system and an induction engine
  • 1891 - Nikola Tesla becomes a naturalized american
  • 1893 - Invents the Tesla Polyphase System
  • 1895 - Loses all of his early research due to a fire
  • 1896 - Creates an apparatus able to generate x-rays
  • 1898 - Makes the first demonstration of his Teleautomaton: a radio-controlled boat. Nobody believes him, the crowd that witnessed his demonstration claimed the boat was piloted by a trained monkey inside.
    In the same year he causes an earthquake in New York with his electro-mechanical oscillator
  • 1899 - Tesla produces an artificial lightning in Colorado Springs, with discharges consisting of millions of volts and up to 135 feet long
  • 1900 - Begins planning the Wardenclyffe Tower facility, unfortunately his project will be abandoned in 1906
  • 1906 - On his 50th birthday demonstrates his bladeless turbine
  • 1912 - Postulated that electricity could be used to locate submarines via using the reflection of an "electric ray" of "tremendous frequency," with the signal being viewed on a fluorescent screen
  • 1928 - Tesla receives his last patent for a biplane capable of taking off vertically and then be gradually tilted through manipulation of the elevator devices in flight until it was flying like a conventional plane
  • 1935 - Announces a method of transmitting mechanical energy with minimal loss over any terrestrial distance, a related new means of communication, and a method of accurately determining the location of underground mineral deposits.
  • 1943 - At the age of 86, on 7 January, Nikola Tesla died alone in room 3327 of the New Yorker Hotel for a coronary thrombosis.
  • 1900 to Death - Tesla worked on the Teleforce, also known as death ray; a weapon that could bring down a fleet of 10,000 enemy airplanes at a distance of 200 miles
